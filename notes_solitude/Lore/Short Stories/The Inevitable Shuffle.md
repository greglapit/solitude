The Spread is a universe structured around a deck of cards. Within this universe, four ancient and historically powerful factions coexist and contend with one another. They are known as the Suits: the Diamonds, Hearts, Clubs, and Spades. Within each Suit exists a complete and functioning society. These societies interact with one another in ways familiar to any world. They trade, communicate, cooperate, and coexist. More than anything else, however, they wage war.

The Diamonds, Hearts, Clubs, and Spades each possess their own defining strengths. The Diamonds are renowned for their devotion to craftsmanship. They are defined by their ability to perceive potential and forcibly give it form. The Hearts possess a natural affinity for emotion and perspective. They value balance, moderation, and purpose, strengthened by their religious devotion, and are perpetually burdened by a longing for absolution. The Clubs represent dominion over nature, growth, and raw vitality. They seek harmony through control, elevating absolute natural authority as guiding principles. The Spades are bound to power and conflict. They specialize in intuition and the subconscious, exerting control through shadow, influence, and persuasion rather than overt force.

The history of the Spread is scarred by continual large-scale conflict between nations, to such a degree that warfare itself has become refined and ritualized. To classify threats and respond to them appropriately, nations created the Rank System. Every individual within the Suits is assigned a rank based on their capacity for combat. The vast majority of the population exists as unranked, designated as Rank Zero. These are typical civilians with little to no martial ability. Those who possess rank, most often serving the governing bodies of their respective Suits, are known as Cards. Cards are assigned ranks between one and ten. Rank One Cards are uncommon, Rank Two rarer, and increasingly so as you travel upwards the system. Upper-rank Cards are exceedingly rare,  their power obscured as a final advantage against unsuspecting enemies.

Above the numbered ranks exist three singular entities known as the Faces. These are the Jack, the Queen, and the King. Unlike numbered ranks, each Face title is held by only one singular individual. The Faces represent the pinnacle of power within a Suit and typically rule their people for the entirety of their existence. Their power is inconceivable—manipulating battlefields and changing the tides of war in ways not thought possible.

In combat, Cards draw upon abilities granted to them by the nature of their Suit. Cards and threats of a given rank are expected to reliably eliminate those ranked beneath them. A Four card should have no issue dealing with a Two. However, as the difference in rank narrows (i.e. a rank Four vs. Five), combat becomes increasingly dangerous and outcomes are unpredictable. Encounters between Cards of equal rank are volatile and there is no assured victor.

The Diamonds are distinct in that they have augmented their martial power through the creation of extraordinary weapons. While Cards of other Suits channel abilities intrinsic to their nature, Diamonds manifest their strength through physical craft. Cards of the Diamond Suit wield unmistakably otherworldly weapons. These weapons draw their power from diamonds embedded directly within them. The number of diamonds set into a weapon determines its rank and classification. Common examples include Rank Two daggers with twin shards embedded within each hilt, or Rank Three tridents formed with three diamonds crowning the tips of their prongs.

# Word Lore

Viewed from an existential perspective, the Spread itself is metaphorically understood as a deck of cards. The state of a deck of cards depends entirely upon the act of shuffling. Without shuffling, reality begins to crystallize. This *stagnation* is felt by all who inhabit the Spread. In the absence of reshuffling, the world slowly withers. Stagnation is death to a deck of cards. Cards are meant to be shuffled and played, not left untouched. The Spread endures an inescapable descent into stasis until the universe is shuffled once more. A shuffle acts as a reset. After a shuffle, societies are reborn and history is retold. The Suits rise again, construct their cities, amass their armies, and clash with neighboring powers, bound eternally to repeat the same progression.

As generations pass as societies mature, two extraterrestrial beings emerge. Whether they have existed unseen since the beginning or deliberately time their arrival remains unknown, but their appearance always coincides precisely with the approach of a new shuffle and the slow onset of stagnation. One of these beings is the player. The player is the Red Joker, the Fool, an entity that embodies beginnings and renewal. Alongside the Fool is born the Black Joker, the World, an antagonist who represents endings and completion. These two are opposite sides of the same coin. The Fool is burdened with a curse of amnesia, and the World is cursed with omniscience. As the Spread approaches its end, these two beings possess the power to initiate a shuffle, resetting reality and liberating its inhabitants from decaying into stagnation.

The Red Joker is born unaware of their curse. Each shuffle grants them a blank existence, reborn without memory of prior lives despite having lived through thousands. The Black Joker exists in stark contrast. They are painfully conscious of both the Spread's affliction, as well as the endless cycles they are forced to endure. Their relationship is deeply fractured. One bears absolute knowledge, while the other carries none. The Red and Black Jokers are born among the red and black Suits, respectively. The Red Joker, unburdened by memory, gradually comes to sense the stagnation of the Spread. As the Fool, driven by an instinctual need to begin anew, they seek a cure for the universe's stagnation, as they have done countless times before. The Fool, with a natural affinity towards the red suits, seeks the help of the Diamonds and their powerful weapons to begin their journey. What they discover is fierce resistance. The Spread itself works against them. They are encountering opposition in the from the decaying Cards themselves. Those who would seemingly benefit most from reshuffling mysteriously oppose its completion.

The World seeks a definitive end. Driven by their nature as an embodiment of completion, the World pursues a future in which shuffling ceases entirely. They see all possible futures and dedicate themselves to one alone. A future where both Jokers exist without their curse of the Spread shuffling. If shuffling were to end, the World would no longer be bound to enforce predetermined outcomes, no longer a mechanism of inevitability. The Fool would no longer forget, would finally be able to cherish relationships, retain meaning, and remember their bonds—a sacrifice they never hesitated to make. The World is willing to sacrifice the universe itself to protect the two of them, against the wishes of the unassuming Fool. The World turns the Spread against the Fool, severing all paths that might lead to reconciliation. Only through the pain of keeping the Fool away will the Jokers be able to enjoy a future. A diminished future, yet spared.

Thus unfolds the tragedy. An infinite cycle that the Fool unknowingly sustains, and the World desperately resists. The Fool, cursed with forgetfulness, remains unaware of their imprisonment within this endless repetition, destined to perpetuate it without end. The World fights to end the Fool’s suffering by restraining the Spread and preventing reshuffling altogether. The Fool fights for the Spread and unknowingly against themselves. The World fights for them both. Each is correct within its own nature. 

We parallel these actions with a physical deck of cards. Beyond the fourth wall, a shuffle typically occurs only after the Jokers are removed from the deck. An act mirrored in the Spread by the death of the Fool and the World, which is revealed to be necessary for the universe’s prolonging. The recombination of all cards in a deck after play leads to the reinsertion of the Jokers, marking their rebirth. As long as a deck of cards is used for play, a shuffle is inevitable. The World regrettably knows this is unavoidable. The World sees every future laid bare before them, and none untainted by their curses—yet still they fight for their happiness. However futile, however endless.
